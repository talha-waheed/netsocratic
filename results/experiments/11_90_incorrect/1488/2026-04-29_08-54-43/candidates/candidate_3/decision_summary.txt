Reasoning:
1. Brussels needs to reach 100.0.9.0/24 (Rotterdam) and all paths reaching this should go through Rotterdam as a mandatory waypoint.
2. The candidates for reaching 100.0.9.0/24 from Brussels are:
   - Brussels → Rotterdam (cost 1)
   - Brussels → Amsterdam → Rotterdam (cost 1 + 1 = 2)
3. Since Amsterdam is in the path to 100.0.9.0/24, we need to adjust the cost for the alternative route via Amsterdam to exceed the required metric for the mandatory waypoint through Rotterdam.
4. The cost from Brussels to those bypass paths must be raised. To enforce that the traffic goes through Rotterdam and ensure that the traffic can be balanced across 2 equal-cost paths, the cost from Brussels to Rotterdam is kept at 1 while the alternate route is set to 1000.
5. Noted that exactly 2 equal-cost paths are kept since other paths to 100.0.9.0/24 from Warsaw and Brussels must also go through Rotterdam.
