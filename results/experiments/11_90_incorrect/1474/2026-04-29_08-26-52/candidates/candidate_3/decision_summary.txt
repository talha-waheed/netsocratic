Routers modified: marseille, basel, sofia, kiev

Reasoning:
- For the route from marseille to 100.0.4.0/24, there's a necessary waypoint through basel.
  To ensure this, the cost to any bypass path (through other routers) must be set to 1000, while the route going through basel is kept at cost 1.
- For the route from sofia to 100.0.29.0/24, the way must go through london. All bypass paths must also be kept at a high cost to enforce this required routing.
- Since the pathway from basel to 100.0.1.0/24 needs to utilize kiev as a waypoint, the routing paths bypassing kiev are set to a cost of 1000. The designated path via kiev can be at cost 1 ensuring that it is preferred.
- The connection from kiev to 100.0.1.0/24 must balance across two paths, so the cost to the other potential bypass paths was set at 10 to allow only two viable options.

Limitations (if any):
All bypass paths for waypoint enforcement were satisfactorily handled. The load balancing for basel couldn't yield more than two equal-cost paths due to the absence of multiple peers towards 100.0.1.0/24 from connected subnets.
