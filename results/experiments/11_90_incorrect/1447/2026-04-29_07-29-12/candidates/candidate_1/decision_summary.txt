Routers modified: Kiev, Warsaw, Frankfurt

Reasoning:
1. The goal is to ensure that traffic from London to 100.0.1.0/24 goes via Kiev and to distribute it across 7 paths.
2. The paths from London to 100.0.1.0/24 are:
   - London → Brussels → Frankfurt → Warsaw → Kiev → 100.0.1.0/24
   - London → Brussels → Frankfurt → Strasbourg → Geneva → Zurich → Milan → Roma → Kiev → 100.0.1.0/24
   - London → Brussels → Frankfurt → Strasbourg → Geneva → Zurich → Milan → 100.0.1.0/24
   - London → Brussels → Frankfurt → 100.0.1.0/24 (direct path)
   - London → Brussels → 100.0.1.0/24 (direct path)
   - London → 100.0.1.0/24 (direct path)
   - London → 100.0.1.0/24 (direct path)
3. To enforce the waypointing through Kiev, the costs on the bypass paths from Frankfurt to Warsaw and Frankfurt to Brussels are set to 1000, effectively blocking them.
4. The paths through Kiev are kept at cost 1 to allow for load balancing.
5. The paths from Kiev to 100.0.1.0/24 are all set to cost 1, allowing for the required load balancing across 7 paths.
