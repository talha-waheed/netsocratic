Routers modified: Amsterdam, Brussels, Frankfurt, Geneva, Budapest, Bratislava, Zagreb

Reasoning:
1. Each target router must connect to various subnets as specified in the routing rules.
2. The connections from Amsterdam, Brussels, and Frankfurt to 100.0.4.0/24 (Basel) are routed via Basel.
3. Other paths bypass Basel, necessitating a high cost (1000) to enforce the waypoint requirement on the connection.
4. Therefore, costs are set to 1000 on all bypass paths and 1 on the Basel path.
5. Each router modification maintains the connectivity requirement and load-balanced paths according to prescribed policies.

Limitations (if any):
