Routers modified: Frankfurt, Rotterdam, Basel

Reasoning:
1. Starting with Frankfurt needing to reach subnet 100.0.1.0/24 from London with 6 equal-cost paths.
2. Multiple paths are required to ensure load balancing. Given that Frankfurt to London must go via Rotterdam as a waypoint (costs need to be adjusted).
3. Set costs on all bypassing paths from Frankfurt to Rotterdam to be prohibitively high (1000).
4. For the Rotterdam router, paths and costs were adjusted such that it permits a high cost on bypass paths, allowing the path through Frankfurt to remain effective.
5. Basel was also invoked as a waypoint necessity for Lyon to reach 100.0.4.0/24; hence costs were similarly adjusted.
6. Configurations on these routers ensure compliance with waypointing, reachability, and load balancing throughout the adjusted routing paths.
