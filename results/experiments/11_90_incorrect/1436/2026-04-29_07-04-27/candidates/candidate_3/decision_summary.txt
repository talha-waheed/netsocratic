Routers modified: Amsterdam, Bucharest, Frankfurt, Kiev, Paris

Reasoning:
1. For traffic from Paris to 100.0.1.0/24, the required mandatory waypoint is Kiev. 
   To ensure that traffic goes through Kiev, all bypass paths (like Paris to Frankfurt) must be set to a high cost (1000).
2. Subsequently, we need to ensure there are exactly 2 equal-cost paths available for this traffic. 
   Only the paths that include Kiev will be kept at a low cost (1), hence the modifications.
3. For traffic from London to 100.0.4.0/24 via Basel, similar logic applies. Bypass paths need to be set high (cost 1000).
4. Modifications to the OSPF costs ensure that mandatory waypoints are respected and path counts are correctly balanced.
5. All other routers remain unchanged, as their configurations meet the routing requirements.

Limitations (if any):
