Routers modified: amsterdam, bratislava, barcelona, frankfurt, praha, sofia, strasbourg

Reasoning:
1. **Reachability**: Each router was assessed for reachability to their respective destination prefixes.
2. **Waypointing**:
   - For `bratislava` to `100.0.9.0/24` through `rotterdam`, the cost on paths bypassing `rotterdam` was set to 1000.
   - For `barcelona` to `100.0.4.0/24` through `basel`, paths not going through `basel` were blocked with a cost of 1000.
   - For `amsterdam` to `100.0.29.0/24`, the path through `london` needed to be prioritized, thus costs on bypass paths were heightened.
3. **Load Balancing**:
   - Pairs like (`praha`, `100.0.9.0/24`), (`strasbourg`, `100.0.4.0/24`), etc., required exactly 2 paths; costs for non-conforming routes were raised to ensure only two equal cost paths exist.
   - All necessary costs were calculated to form distinct and allowable paths, maintaining the required balance according to the topology constraints.
4. All modifications adhered strictly to routing rules without altering any other configurations.
