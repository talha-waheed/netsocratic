Routers modified: [Brussels, Kiev]

Reasoning:
1. **Reachability**: Brussels must reach 100.0.1.0/24. This is ensured by the OSPF configuration which redistributes connected subnets.
2. **Waypoint**: Traffic from Brussels to 100.0.1.0/24 must pass through Kiev. To enforce this, the cost of the link from Brussels to any other potential bypass routes to 100.0.1.0/24 is set to 1000, effectively blocking those paths.
3. **Load Balancing**: Brussels must load balance traffic to 100.0.1.0/24 across exactly 4 equal-cost paths. The configuration allows for multiple paths through Kiev, ensuring that the paths through Kiev are set to a cost of 1, while all other paths are set to 1000, thus achieving the required load balancing.
