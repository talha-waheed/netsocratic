Routers modified: brussels, kiev, marseille

Reasoning:
1. Brussels must reach 100.0.1.0/24, with Kiev as a mandatory waypoint and 4 equal-cost paths.
2. Bypass paths from Brussels to 100.0.1.0/24 do not go through Kiev (i.e., to Warsaw) need to be blocked.
3. Therefore, cost 1000 is set on all bypass paths from Brussels (Fa2/1 to Paris, Fa1/0 to Frankfurt, Fa1/1 to London, Fa3/0 to Rotterdam).
4. The cost from Brussels to Kiev (via Fa1/1) is set to 1.
5. For the load balancing requirement, we must ensure 4 ECMP paths from Brussels to 100.0.1.0/24, thus the costs were adjusted to achieve this. Since we can only reach through Kiev, the paths will depend on how we configure the paths from Kiev.
6. Kiev has direct paths to both Bucharest and Warsaw, both of which can reach 100.0.1.0/24.
7. The cost to Bucharest (Fa1/0) and Warsaw (Fa0/1) is set to 1, allowing equal cost paths through either direction.
8. Marseille must also reach 100.0.1.0/24 via Kiev; hence, its costs to bypass Kiev are also set to 1000.
9. The same cost adjustments are made for Marseille to ensure it can reach Kiev effectively.
